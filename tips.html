Energy Saving Tips

Small household changes can make a big difference over time. Use these tips to reduce energy use without making your routine difficult.

Quick Tips to Start With

Turn Off Unused Lights

Switch off lights when leaving a room. This is one of the simplest ways to avoid unnecessary energy use.

Use Natural Light

Open blinds or curtains during the day instead of relying on indoor lighting.

Unplug Devices

Some devices still use power when they are plugged in but not being used. Unplug chargers, game consoles, and small appliances when possible.

Wash Full Loads

Use the washing machine or dishwasher only when there is a full load to reduce both energy and water use.

Appliance-Specific Tips

Air Conditioner

Set the temperature to a comfortable but efficient level. Avoid setting it much colder or hotter than needed, as this can increase energy use.

Fridge and Freezer

Keep fridge doors closed as much as possible. Check that seals are working properly so cold air does not escape.

Computer and TV

Turn off screens when they are not being used. Sleep mode helps, but turning devices off completely saves more energy.

Washing Machine

Use cold water washes where suitable. Heating water can use a large amount of energy.

Easy Daily Habits

  • Check your usage summary once a week.
  • Set one small goal instead of trying to change everything at once.
  • Compare this week’s usage with last week’s usage.
  • Focus on the rooms or appliances used most often.
  • Celebrate small improvements, even if the change is minor.

Advanced Ways to Use This Website

Use the Dashboard Regularly

The dashboard can help you quickly see changes in your household energy use. Checking it regularly makes it easier to notice patterns.

Compare Weekly Progress

Use the home page summary to compare this week with last week. This helps show whether your household habits are improving.

Set Realistic Goals

Use the goals page to choose small and realistic goals, such as reducing energy use by 5% or turning off unused devices each night.

Look for Patterns

If usage increases, think about what changed that week. For example, extra laundry, air conditioning, gaming, or cooking may explain the increase.

Keep It Simple

You do not need to change everything at once. Start with one or two tips, check your progress, and build better habits over time.
